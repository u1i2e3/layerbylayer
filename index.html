Why AI Is the Best Search Engine

How AI is transforming the way we find information.

WHAT IS ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE?

AI can be thought of as a technological brain that learns through a process called machine learning, which involves using examples. AI systems are fed large amounts of information with the help of humans and can learn to distinguish between, for instance, a dog and a cat by analyzing many pictures of each. These images allow the AI to associate specific physical traits with each species. For example, an image of an animal with four legs could be either a dog or a cat. But if the animal is small, the possibilities narrow it might be a cat or a chihuahua. If the animal in the photo also has whiskers, it’s more likely to be a cat. This is how AI learns: by analyzing a wide variety of data to identify patterns and make sense of new information. A more user friendly way to describe AI is to think of it as a digital person that knows most of the information available on the internet.

Personalization

Personalization plays a major role in the world of AI, as an AI can be taught to perform specific tasks rather than trying to do everything. For example, an AI that is trained exclusively in mathematics will likely provide better and more accurate mathematical answers compared to one trained on general topics. However, the math-focused AI might be harder to understand, as it may use a more technical tone that reflects the data it was trained on.

Limitations & Realism

AI is excellent when it comes to facts and well established information such as the power rule in calculus which is straightforward and technical, making it easier for a computer to process and understand. However, a major challenge arises when dealing with reasoning and more emotional or subjective aspects. AI does not have personal experiences; it simply draws from the information available on the internet. For example, an AI can be described as someone that writes notes for a class, but it doesn’t truly understand the content. Instead, it pulls from countless notes and resources to fill in the gaps in order to fully undestand the content of the notes. Meaning that information is needed in order to understand his own information, something that humans can deduce by simply looking at something. But what happens if there aren't enough notes or reliable information available? That’s where a major issue arises—AI hallucinations. This occurs when an AI generates incorrect or entirely made up information. Hallucinations are one of the biggest problems with AI today, as the spread of false or fabricated facts can have serious consequences if not addressed properly.
